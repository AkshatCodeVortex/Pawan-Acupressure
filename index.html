Our Therapy

Janu Basti

Janu Basti is a traditional Ayurvedic therapy focusing on the knee joint. Warm medicated oil is held within a dough dam on the knee, alleviating pain, promoting flexibility, and nourishing the joint.

Cup Therapy

Cup therapy, also known as cupping, is a traditional healing technique that involves placing suction cups on the skin to stimulate blood flow, reduce muscle tension, and promote overall well-being.

Kansa Thali

Kansa Thali is a traditional Indian dinner plate made of bronze or bell metal. Known for its health benefits, it is believed to balance the body's doshas and improve digestion.

Kati Vasti

Kati Vasti is a traditional Ayurvedic therapy that involves retaining warm herbal oil over the lower back area, particularly around the lumbar region. This therapeutic treatment is renowned for alleviating lower back pain, addressing spinal issues, and promoting overall flexibility and well-being.

Acupressure And Massage Therapy

1.Stress Relief:Acupressure and massage are known for their ability to reduce stress and promote relaxation. The therapeutic touch helps release tension, both physically and mentally.
2.Pain Management:These therapies are effective in managing various types of pain, including muscle soreness, tension headaches, and chronic pain conditions. They promote better circulation and help alleviate discomfort.
3.Improved Circulation:Massage and acupressure techniques stimulate blood flow, enhancing the delivery of oxygen and nutrients to different parts of the body. This can contribute to better overall health.
4.Muscle Tension Release:Both therapies target muscle knots and tension points, helping to release tightness and improve flexibility. This is particularly beneficial for individuals with sedentary lifestyles or those who engage in repetitive motions.
5.Accelerated Recovery from Injuries: Massage therapy is often used as part of rehabilitation programs for injuries. It can aid in the healing process by reducing inflammation, improving range of motion, and preventing scar tissue formation.
